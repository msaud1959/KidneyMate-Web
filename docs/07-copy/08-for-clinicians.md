## 2. The problem in your clinic [rich_prose + stat_band]

**Heading:** The blind spot you already know about

**Body:**
Between appointments, your view of a CKD patient is recall and pathology. Adherence is self-reported under social pressure. Symptom histories compress months into minutes. The patterns that would change your management, the slow fluid creep, the adherence slide after a regimen change, the mood and sleep deterioration that precedes disengagement, form precisely where nobody can see them.

Patient-generated health data was supposed to fix this. Mostly, it hasn't: consumer trackers produce unstructured noise with no clinical pathway, and alert-generating systems without accountable review create work and risk rather than value. The failure mode isn't a lack of data. It's the absence of a structured mechanism for turning that data into clinically accountable feedback. That's the specific problem our framework was written to address.

## 3. What KidneyMate gives you [feature_grid, clinician set]

**Heading:** Signal, structured for review

- **Longitudinal summaries.** Multi-domain patient records (symptoms, adherence, fluid, diet, sleep, mood, dialysis) organised across 90-day views, not scrollable diaries.
- **Trends against the patient's own baseline.** Change from usual, not deviation from population averages, surfaced with the underlying records attached.
- **Review flags with context.** Rule-based, threshold-driven flags you can inspect: what fired, why, and against which patient-specific rule. No black-box scores.
- **Patient-specific rules and targets.** You define fluid limits, adherence thresholds and monitoring rules per patient. Your review decisions feed back into how future records are interpreted.
- **Appointment-ready reports.** The between-visit story in the first minute of the consultation, exportable and shareable with the patient's consent.

## 4. Designed around your workflow [icon_points]

**Heading:** Built to respect the ten-minute reality

- **You set the rules.** Analysis runs on clinician-defined targets and guideline-informed defaults (KDIGO-aligned context, CKD stage, eGFR), tuned per patient.
- **A review queue, not alert spam.** Flags are batched, prioritised and contextualised for scheduled review. Designed against alert fatigue from the first sketch.
- **Nothing autonomous reaches the patient.** Patient-facing notes are self-management insights in plain, non-alarming language. Clinical interpretation and advice remain yours.
- **Accountability is traceable.** Reviews, decisions and target changes are logged. Who saw what, when, and what they decided.
- **Minutes, not hours.** The dashboard is built for the reality of clinic time: triage in seconds, depth on demand.

## 5. Evidence and governance [evidence_teaser + icon_points]

**Heading:** The paperwork you'd want us to have

- **A published framework.** KidneyMate implements "A Conceptual Framework for Chronic Kidney Disease Self-Management Support" (HIKM 2026): data layer, rule-based analysis layer, clinician review layer with a feedback loop.
- **A validation pathway, stated publicly.** Expert review, pilot implementation, and evaluation against usability, interpretability, clinical relevance, transparency, workflow integration and stakeholder acceptance.
- **Privacy by design.** Australian Privacy Principles alignment, consent-based sharing, encryption in transit and at rest, minimal collection. Detail on the Privacy & Trust page.
- **Honest claims only.** KidneyMate is decision support in development. We make no outcome claims ahead of validation, and this website never will.

## 7. What a pilot looks like [numbered_steps]

**Heading:** Piloting with us, step by step

1. **A conversation.** You register interest, we talk about your clinic or service, your CKD cohort, and whether there's a genuine fit.
2. **Scoping.** Together we define the pilot's shape: cohort size, duration, workflows touched, what "useful" would mean for you.
3. **Governance first.** Ethics, privacy, data governance and clinical safety arrangements agreed before a single patient record moves.
4. **A small, supported cohort.** Patients onboard with proper consent; your team gets direct access to us, not a ticketing system.
5. **Co-designed evaluation.** We measure what matters (usability, workflow fit, signal quality, patient engagement) and publish what we learn, with your input on what's shareable.
