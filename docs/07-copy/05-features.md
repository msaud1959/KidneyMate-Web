## 3. Track [feature_grid, category=track]

**Heading:** Track your day, without it taking over your day

**Intro:** Small entries, big picture. Each tracker works alone, and they're better together.

- **Fluid intake** *(tint: fluid, status: in prototype)*
  Log drinks in taps, not typing, against the daily target set with your care team. See where you are at a glance, adjust as the day unfolds.

- **Food and diet** *(tint: food, status: in prototype)*
  Record meals and keep an eye on what matters in a renal diet: potassium, phosphorus, sodium, protein. Guidance stays general and gentle; your dietitian stays your dietitian.

- **Medications** *(tint: medications, status: in prototype)*
  Your schedule, with reminders that respect your routine. Mark doses taken, see what's missed, and keep an accurate record for your next appointment instead of a guess.

- **Symptoms** *(tint: symptoms, status: in prototype)*
  Quick check-ins for the symptoms that matter in CKD: fatigue, swelling, breathlessness, nausea, itching, pain and more, rated in seconds so changes show up over time.

- **Sleep** *(tint: sleep_mood, status: in prototype)*
  How long, how well. Sleep shapes symptoms and mood, and its patterns are worth seeing next to everything else.

- **Mood and emotions** *(tint: sleep_mood, status: in prototype)*
  A gentle daily check-in, because living with a chronic condition is emotional work, and because mood changes can be an early part of a bigger pattern.

- **Physical activity** *(tint: symptoms, status: in development)*
  Light-touch activity logging, focused on your functional day rather than gym metrics.

- **Appointments** *(tint: appointments, status: in prototype)*
  GP, specialist, pathology and dialysis appointments in one place, with reminders and space for the questions you want to remember to ask.

- **Dialysis sessions** *(tint: appointments, status: in development)*
  Session reminders and simple logging for people on dialysis, adding context that makes the rest of your records more meaningful.

- **Personalised reminders** *(tint: neutral, status: in prototype)*
  Nudges for medications, fluids, check-ins and appointments, tuned to your day. Quiet by design: reminders you can live with are reminders you keep.

## 4. Understand [feature_grid, category=understand]

**Heading:** From records to sense

- **Trends dashboard** *(status: in prototype)*
  Your weeks at a glance: fluid, symptoms, adherence, sleep and mood, side by side, so patterns stop hiding in separate places.

- **Your personal baseline** *(status: in prototype)*
  KidneyMate learns what usual looks like for you, and measures change against that, not against a population average that was never you.

- **Plain-language insights** *(status: in prototype)*
  Not scores or jargon. Short, calm notes about what's changing and why it might be worth attention.

- **Every insight shows its working** *(status: in prototype)*
  Tap "why am I seeing this?" on any insight and get the rule behind it in plain words. If we can't explain it simply, we don't ship it.

- **Gentle by design** *(status: in prototype)*
  No red sirens, no guilt streaks, no doom graphs. Information arrives the way a good nurse would say it.

## 5. Connect [feature_grid, category=connect]

**Heading:** The bridge to your care team

- **Appointment-ready summaries** *(status: in development)*
  Turn months of records into a clear, printable summary for your next consultation. Ten minutes with your nephrologist goes a lot further when the story arrives organised.

- **Clinician review flags** *(status: in development)*
  With your consent, patterns worth professional eyes reach your care team as flags with context, feeding the review process rather than replacing it.

- **Targets set with your care team** *(status: in prototype)*
  Fluid limits, monitoring rules and personal thresholds defined by the people who know your care plan, so the analysis fits you.

- **Clinician dashboard** *(status: in development)*
  A calm, structured view for clinicians: trends, flags and summaries across their enrolled patients. Built with clinicians, headed for pilot programs.

- **Health app integration** *(status: planned)*
  Connections to the health data already on your phone, where it genuinely adds context.

- **Conversational guidance** *(status: planned)*
  Everyday questions ("what should I watch with takeaway pizza?") answered within safe, clearly bounded limits, never contradicting your care plan, never giving medical advice. Arrives only when we can do it responsibly.

- **Health system integration** *(status: planned)*
  EHR and My Health Record pathways, subject to validation research and the governance work that has to come first.

## 6. Feature spotlights [split_content x3]

**Spotlight 1: The fluid ring** *(visual: fluid tracker screen)*
**Heading:** A limit that finally feels manageable
**Body:** Your daily fluid target, set with your care team, as a simple ring that fills as you log. Quick-add buttons for your usual cup, glass or mug. On hot days, on dialysis days, you always know where you stand, without arithmetic at the kitchen bench.
**Alt text:** Illustration of the KidneyMate fluid tracker showing a progress ring at 70 percent of a personal daily target.

**Spotlight 2: The insight that explains itself** *(visual: insight card screen)*
**Heading:** "Why am I seeing this?" is always one tap away
**Body:** Example: "Your records show several missed evening doses this week. This can matter for your care plan, and your clinician may review it." Tap through and KidneyMate shows the plain rule behind the note. That's the whole trick: no mystery, no alarm, just a clear reason.
**Alt text:** Illustration of a KidneyMate insight card with an explanation panel showing the rule that produced it.

**Spotlight 3: The appointment summary** *(visual: clinician trends screen, browser frame)*
**Heading:** Walk in with the story, not just the memory
**Body:** Months of fluid, symptoms, medications, sleep and mood, organised into the view a clinician needs in the first two minutes of a consultation. What changed, when, and what's been steady. Your appointment time goes to decisions, not reconstruction.
**Alt text:** Illustration of a KidneyMate summary view showing multi-domain trends across ninety days.
