# 01. Website Positioning Strategy

## The one-line position

KidneyMate is a daily companion for people living with chronic kidney disease, and a clearer window into daily life for the clinicians who care for them.

## The narrative spine: the days between appointments

Kidney care happens in clinics for a few hours a year. Living with kidney disease happens every single day. Fluid limits, renal diets, medication schedules, symptoms that are hard to describe, sleep that will not come, moods that dip without warning. All of that work falls on the patient, alone, in the gap between appointments, and almost none of it is visible to the people responsible for their care.

Every page of the website hangs off this one idea. The problem is the gap. The solution is a supported daily routine that closes it. The features are the tools that make the routine possible. The clinician story is the other side of the same gap. The research is the proof the gap is real and the approach is sound. Privacy and trust are the conditions for anyone letting a product into that gap at all.

This gives the site a story to tell rather than a feature list to recite, which is the single biggest defence against feeling generic or template-built.

## What the site must achieve, per audience

The site serves three audiences at once. It does this by leading with the patient story (emotionally strongest, universally understandable) and layering clinical and strategic credibility immediately behind it, plus dedicated pages for each audience.

### Patients and carers
- Feel understood within the first screen. The daily burden is described the way it is actually lived, not the way a brochure describes it.
- Understand what KidneyMate does in plain language within one scroll.
- Trust that it is safe, private, and connected to real care, not another app shouting notifications.
- Act: join the waitlist.

### Clinicians and health services
- Recognise the clinical visibility gap as a real problem in their own practice.
- See that KidneyMate is decision support with human accountability, built on a peer-reviewed framework, not an autonomous adviser or another alert-fatigue machine.
- See workflow respect: clinicians set targets, review flags, and stay in charge.
- Act: register pilot interest.

### Investors, researchers and stakeholders
- Grasp the scale (2.7 million Australian adults with signs of kidney disease, 788 million adults worldwide) and the timing (rising burden, maturing patient-generated health data practice, supportive evidence base).
- See a credible, staged pathway: peer-reviewed framework, working prototype, validation with around 50 stakeholders, planned clinical validation research.
- See responsible design as a strategic asset, not a compliance afterthought.
- Act: get in touch.

## Positioning pillars

Every claim on the site ladders up to one of these four pillars.

1. **One steady place for the daily work.** KidneyMate gathers the scattered jobs of CKD self-management (fluids, food, medications, symptoms, sleep, mood, activity, appointments, dialysis sessions) into one calm routine.

2. **Sense, not just numbers.** KidneyMate compares each person's records against their own baseline and their care team's targets, then explains what it notices in plain language. Transparent rules first; every insight can show its working.

3. **Your care team, in the loop.** Records become structured summaries and review flags that clinicians can actually use. People, not software, make every clinical decision. This is the framework's clinician review layer, and it is the sharpest differentiator against generic health trackers.

4. **Trust built in, not bolted on.** Privacy by design under the Australian Privacy Principles, explainable analysis, human oversight, and a research-first pathway to validation.

## Differentiation

The honest competitive claim, taken from the pitch deck and defensible in public: KidneyMate is not another generic health tracker.

- CKD-specific by design: renal-relevant tracking domains and guideline-informed context (CKD stage, eGFR, clinician-set targets), not step counts with a kidney logo.
- Longitudinal, not episodic: patterns against a personal baseline across weeks and months, exactly where single data points mislead.
- Patient-to-clinician visibility: structured summaries and review flags, a pathway no consumer tracker offers.
- A focused CKD ecosystem with a published framework behind it and a stated path to clinical validation.

Competitors are never named on the website. The comparison is drawn against "generic trackers" and "the paper diary and memory" status quo.

## Honesty constraints (non-negotiable)

- KidneyMate is in development. Present tense is allowed for what the prototype does; futures and conditionals for everything on the roadmap (clinician dashboard at scale, EHR and My Health Record integration, conversational guidance, AI features).
- The framework is peer reviewed; the product is not yet clinically validated. The site says so plainly and treats the validation roadmap as a strength.
- No health outcome promises. Evidence from the literature (for example, supported self-management improving self-efficacy) is attributed to the literature, not to KidneyMate.
- Not a medical device claim, not medical advice, not for emergencies. Standing disclaimer in the footer and on patient-facing pages.

## Conversion strategy

One primary conversion for the whole site: the waitlist, segmented by role at the form (patient or carer, clinician, researcher or partner). Clinician pilot interest and research collaboration route through the same form with different interest options, so all demand lands in one measurable place.

Secondary conversions: reading the research paper, and the contact form for media, partnership and investment conversations.

## Success measures for the site

- Waitlist signups, segmented by role.
- Pilot interest registrations from clinicians or clinics.
- Qualitative: does a nephrologist, a patient, and a grant assessor each find their page and their proof within two clicks. The sitemap is built so the answer is yes.
